Shoulder Design Process
-
My first Version was a parallel arms style gripper with 2 gears attached to arms that are moved by one servo, this design looked cool but overall had too much slop. Even though it could hold on to things effectivly, the far end of the fingers were a bit weak, it was hard on the servo and the 8 M3 screws nuts would unscrew themselves automatically when the gripper was used, and i didnt want to permenenly attatch them with lovktight because then the servo would be stuck in there forever.

V1 Problems
- weak towards far end of fingers
- hard on servo

For my second version I made 2 free moving fingers on either side of the center wrist mount that were attached via 3d printed pins. The plan was to have a rubber band around the 2 fingers and the servo would be in the middle and extend horizontally to open it and go vertical to close. Unfortunately even if I used the smallest rubber band I could find, it was too much for the servo to open reliably.

V2 Problems
- too much force on servo
- broken servo

For the 3rd Version I slightly modified the V2 but added a rack and pinon style gear set up to do the open/closing mechanism and mounted the rack to one finger and mounted the other finger to the side of thewrist mount.

V3 problems
- only has one set distance to open to atm
- Possible solutions
- force sensor on inside of fingers
